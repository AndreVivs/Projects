The best 5 netflix documentaries for designers

NETFIX

The streaming video service is packed with inspiring documentaries on all sorts of creative topics, perfect for helping you resurrect your imagination and provide you with ideas and inspiration.

The only problem is that there is such a huge amount of content on Netflix that the best stuff can get lost among the many options. So we've done the hard work for you and put together a collection: The 12 Best Netflix Documentaries For Graphic Designers And Creatives.

01. ABSTRACT: THE ART OF DESIGN

Created by former Wired editor-in-chief Scott Dadich, the documentary series Abstract: The Art of Design runs for two seasons. The first of eight independent profiles of leading designers and creatives. Each one is around 40 minutes long, superbly shot and very well edited.

There is a wealth of information as each artist/creative reveals their process and the challenges they face in their work. In short, if you watch a Netflix show this year, you have to watch this one.

The first season, which first aired in 2017, features graphic designer Paula Scher, illustrator Christoph Niemann, Nike shoe designer Tinker Hatfield, scenic designer Es Devlin, architect Bjarke Ingels, automotive designer Ralph Gilles , the photographer Platon and the interior designer Ilse Crawford.

The second season is already on the air with a new group of luxury creatives that you cannot miss.

02. THE TOYS THAT MADE US

Many of us discover our first real appreciation for design, albeit subconsciously, through interaction with our childhood toys. It is a love that often stays with us throughout our lives. The Toys That Made Us Season 2 Trailer Netflix

View this video on YouTube The quirky documentary series The Toys that Made Us, looking at the making of some of the world's most iconic toy franchises, is a great way to think about design in a nostalgic and familiar way, and provides new insights into what goes into design. making a classic

Its 40-50 minute long episodes investigate everything from Star Trek and Transformers to LEGO and Hello Kitty. In doing so, this series goes beyond the superficial and really delves into how toys are conceived, designed, and manufactured.

03. THE CREATIVE BRAIN

We all would like to be more creative and productive in our work, but often find it difficult to do so in practice. The Creative Brain is an insightful 52-minute documentary whose goal is to help us find a way forward.

To find out how this actually works, he meets with leading practitioners from across the creative spectrum, from ceramics to nanotechnology, and tries to unravel their creative process in a logical way. The results are truly fascinating and will make you think about your graphic design work in a whole new way.

04. THE PIXAR STORY

In the mid-1980s, a trio of Bay Area idealists combined their talents in art, science, and business to launch a company that would define entertainment for the foreseeable future. Those three people were Ed Catmull, John Lasseter, and Steve Jobs. His company was Pixar.

The Pixar Story will take you behind the scenes to witness the creative struggle and determination that pushed animation technology to the next level that revolutionized Hollywood. The film brings together never-before-seen footage and exclusive interviews with key people, including Tim Allen and Tom Hanks, to chronicle Pixar's journey from inception to paradigm shift.

05. MINIMALISM: A DOCUMENTARY ABOUT IMPORTANT THINGS

Right now, minimalism is a huge trend in graphic design, from app interfaces to logos. But what is minimalism really all about, and how can it make your creative ideas better, rather than just make them look like everyone else?

Minimalism is a documentary about the important things where Matt D'Avella examines the roots of minimalism as a reaction to modern consumerism and media overload, and how it is being passed over to new movements such as simplified and ordered ways of living.

It's important to note that this documentary, which is 1 hour and 18 minutes long, doesn't actually talk about the use of minimalism in graphic design. But it will help you better understand where the minimalist trend came from and why it has a cultural resonance in today's society.
